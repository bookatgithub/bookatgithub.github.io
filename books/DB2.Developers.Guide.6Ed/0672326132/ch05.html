You must make many choices when implementing DB2 objects. The large number of alternatives can intimidate the beginning user. By following the data definition guidelines in this chapter, you can ensure that you make the proper physical design decisions. Rules are provided for selecting the appropriate DB2 DDL parameters, choosing the proper DB2 objects for your application, and implementing a properly designed physical database.
